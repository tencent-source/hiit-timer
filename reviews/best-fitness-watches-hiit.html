Buying Guide: What to Look For

1. Heart Rate Accuracy

For HIIT training, accurate heart rate monitoring is crucial. Look for watches with optical heart rate sensors that can track rapid changes during high-intensity intervals. Garmin and Polar are known for the most accurate sensors.

2. Interval Timer Features

The best HIIT watches have built-in interval timers with customizable work/rest periods. Some watches (like Garmin) even have pre-programmed HIIT workouts you can follow.

3. Battery Life

If you train daily, battery life matters. Garmin and Fitbit offer 7+ days, while Apple Watch needs daily charging. Consider your charging habits when choosing.

4. Recovery Metrics

Advanced watches track recovery time, HRV (heart rate variability), and training load. This helps prevent overtraining and optimize your HIIT schedule.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a fitness watch for HIIT?

No, but it helps! A fitness watch tracks your heart rate zones, ensures you're working hard enough during intervals, and monitors recovery. Our free HIIT Timer works great if you don't want to invest in a watch yet.

Which watch has the best interval timer?

Garmin watches have the most advanced interval timer features with customizable work/rest periods, visual alerts, and automatic lap tracking. Apple Watch is great too with third-party apps like Intervals Pro.

Is Apple Watch good for HIIT?

Yes! The Apple Watch Series 9 tracks HIIT workouts accurately and has a dedicated HIIT workout mode. The main downside is battery life (18 hours vs 7 days for Garmin).
