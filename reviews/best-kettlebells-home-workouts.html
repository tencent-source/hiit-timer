Buying Guide: How to Choose a Kettlebell for Home HIIT

1. Adjustable vs Fixed Weight

Adjustable Kettlebells: Save space and money by replacing multiple bells with one unit. Perfect for small home gyms. Downside: bulkier design and not ideal for advanced ballistic movements like snatches.

Fixed Weight Kettlebells: Traditional design, better balance, and more durable. Ideal for serious training but requires buying multiple weights as you progress.

2. What Weight Should You Start With?

Women: Start with 15-20 lbs for swings, 8-12 lbs for Turkish get-ups and overhead work.

Men: Start with 25-35 lbs for swings, 15-20 lbs for overhead movements.

For HIIT workouts, you'll eventually want 2-3 different weights to match exercise difficulty.

3. Handle Width and Comfort

Handle diameter matters for grip comfort during high-rep HIIT sets. Competition kettlebells have standardized 33mm handles. Cast iron bells vary - look for smooth, wide handles (35-38mm) that fit two hands comfortably for swings.

4. Material: Cast Iron vs Competition Steel

Cast Iron: More affordable, varying sizes by weight. Great for home use. Powder coating adds grip but can chip.

Competition Steel: Uniform size across weights, premium feel, better balance. Worth it if you're serious about kettlebell training.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size kettlebell should I buy for HIIT workouts?

For HIIT, most people need 2-3 kettlebells: a lighter one (8-15 lbs) for overhead work and Turkish get-ups, a medium weight (20-30 lbs) for goblet squats and rows, and a heavier bell (35-50 lbs) for swings and deadlifts. An adjustable kettlebell like the Bowflex SelectTech covers all these ranges in one unit.

Are adjustable kettlebells good for HIIT?

Yes, adjustable kettlebells work great for most HIIT exercises like goblet squats, rows, and presses. However, they're bulkier than traditional bells, which can be awkward for advanced ballistic movements like snatches and cleans. For pure HIIT training, they're an excellent space-saving choice.

How many kettlebells do I need for a home gym?

Minimum: 1 medium-weight kettlebell (20-35 lbs depending on strength). Ideal: 3 kettlebells (light, medium, heavy) to match different exercises. Space-saving option: 1 adjustable kettlebell that replaces 6+ fixed weights.
